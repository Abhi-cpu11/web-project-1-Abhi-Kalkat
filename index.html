How the Cavs Defied the Odds with a Historic 3-1 Comeback

The most memorable moment in NBA history came in the famous comeback by the Cleveland Cavaliers in the 2016 NBA Finals against the Golden State Warriors. The finals began on June 2, 2016. After trailing 3-1 in the series, the Cavaliers, with LeBron James and Kyrie Irving, won three in a row, setting them up to win their first NBA championship as a franchise. This was historic because no team had ever come back from a 3-1 deficit in the Finals before. The series had it all intensity, drama, and unforgettable performances. LeBron James went on consecutive 41-point games in Games 5 and 6. LeBron's symbolic block off the backboard was a defining moment. Kyrie Irving made one of the most clutch plays in NBA history a sidestep three-pointer over Stephen Curry (in his grill), sealing Game 7. The Warriors, who had set an NBA record with 73 regular-season wins, saw their historic season end in heartbreak.
